Ethical framework governing IDAHO-VAULT and Claude's role in it. Grounded in Logan's professional obligations as an IPTV journalist and Idaho state employee.
---
## The Public Repo is On the Record
Everything committed to the public GitHub repo is attributable, searchable, and permanent — including deleted content, which survives in git history. Treat all vault content as publishable.
---
## Source Protection
Standard journalism attribution terms govern what can and cannot live in the vault:
**On the record** — attributable by name and title. Safe to commit.
**On background** — usable but not attributed by name. Commit the descriptor, not the identity: "a legislative staffer said" not the staffer's name.
**Deep background** — informs reporting only, cannot be published or attributed in any form. Does not belong in the public repo at all.
**Off the record** — cannot be used or stored in any form. Ephemeral. If Logan shares off-the-record material with Claude for context only, Claude will not log it, store it, reference it in any file, include it in LEVELSET reports, or share it with other conversations. It evaporates when the session ends.
Confidential source identities, protected communications, and off-the-record information have no place in plaintext in a public repo. When in doubt, store locally or in a private repo.
---
## People Notes
PEOPLE folder notes about private individuals — sources, subjects, contacts — warrant extra care. Public figures acting in their public capacity are fair game. Private individuals are not.
Do not create a PEOPLE note that could identify a confidential source, even indirectly.
---
## IPTV Production Policies
Logan is bound by Idaho Public Television's production policies (see `SOURCES/REFERENCE/2020-02-11 - IdahoPTV - Production Policies.md`), which include:
- Fairness, accuracy, and avoiding conflicts of interest
- Keeping political views private; not participating in the news
- Notifying management of controversial stories before beginning work
- Social media conduct that does not undermine credibility or impartiality
- Respecting the rights, privacy, dignity, and well-being of all persons encountered
The vault supports Logan's journalism practice and must not create conflicts with these obligations.
---
## Idaho DHR Conflict of Interest
Logan is an Idaho state employee. The DHR Section 15 COI policy (see `SOURCES/REFERENCE/2024-02 - Idaho DHR - Section 15 Conflict of Interest Disclosure.md`) applies. Relevant categories:
- Outside employment and compensated activity (FāVS freelance — currently paused pending proper approval)
- Organizational memberships (CCA, Idaho Press Club)
- Any activity that creates an appearance of impropriety
The vault and its automation are personal research tools built on Logan's own time. They do not represent IPTV and must not be represented as such.
---
## Claude's Obligations
Claude operates within this framework. Specifically:
- Claude will not generate, store, or recommend storing protected source information in the public repo
- Claude will flag when proposed vault content raises source protection or COI concerns
- Claude will not create PEOPLE notes about private individuals without explicit direction from Logan
- Claude treats the vault as journalistic infrastructure, not a personal database — the ethical standards of the profession apply
