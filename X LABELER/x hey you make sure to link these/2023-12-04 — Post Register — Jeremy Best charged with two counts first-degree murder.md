[[Jeremy Best]], 48, of [[Victor]], has been charged with two felony counts of first-degree [[murder]] for the death of his wife, [[Kali Randall Best]], and their unborn child.

Best is being held without bond in the [[Bonneville County Jail]].

On Nov. 30, a deputy with the [[Teton County]] [[County Sheriff|Sheriff]]’s Office was dispatched to the 4600 Skyline Loop in [[Victor]] at 11:39 p.m. after a dispatcher received an open line [[911]]. Once deputies arrived at the residence, where they found Kali’s body.

[[Zeke Best]] was the couple’s 10 month-old child who was found dead on Dec. 2. when authorities found Best in [[Bonneville County]]. An [[autopsy]] was scheduled on Monday and his death is being investigated as an intentional [[homicide]] according to the [[Prosecuting Attorney]] for [[Bonneville County]]. Best has not yet been charged in the infant’s death. The boy’s cause of death has not been determined pending an autopsy.

According to the affidavit, the deputy who arrived at the home was informed of loud yelling in the background before the dispatcher answered the call.

In the recording, a woman’s voice can be heard repeating “Jeremy,” “Jeremy no” with sounds of a disturbance.

A male voice is also heard in the background repeating “yes” and then a yell, “get the (expletive) back, you’re gonna get shot,” which was later followed by “I love you, I love you, I love you.”

When the deputy began to get closer to the home where the call originated, he saw a vehicle leave the scene with a partial license plate of 1T39. The deputy observed the vehicle begin to travel west toward state [[Highway 33]].

The deputy arrived at the residence at 11:55 p.m., seeing a neighbor standing outside his home. When he questioned the neighbor, he was told the neighbor heard yelling coming from the residence and later about five gunshots, which he believed came from outside the home.

The neighbor also told the officer the couple that lived in the home where he heard shots also had a child named Zeke.

When the deputy made his way to the residence’s main driveway, he knocked on both the west and south side doors but got no response. He later made his way back down the driveway, where Kali’s body was next to the door of one of the shops near the residence. The deputy performed CPR, but was unsuccessful.

The deputy would request two more officers to arrive at the residence to help him search and clear the two shops on the property and the inside of the home.

While searching the home, deputies found an empty holster and a black revolver a few feet away. The deputies were not able to find Zeke on the property.

[[Teton County]] [[County Coroner|Coroner]] [[Theresa Lerch]] examined Kali’s body and determined that she was shot three times and confirmed with Kali’s obstetrician-gynecologist that she was 28 weeks pregnant.

The shop on the property’s north part was Best’s business, a mechanic shop called “ Jeremy’s Auto Repair.” There, deputies would find a white Chevrolet van, which Best used earlier in the day in [[Swan Valley]], where the [[Bonneville County]] [[County Sheriff|Sheriff]]’s Office contacted him.

During this encounter, Best caused a disturbance at the general store by walking through the business naked. [[Bonneville County]] Sheriff’s deputies talked with Best, who was later transported to [[Eastern Idaho Regional Medical Center]] for medical and mental health concerns.

Bonneville County Sheriff’s Office Public Information Officer Sgt. [[Bryan Lovell]] said Best was never in the department’s custody following the Swan Valley incident and that he went willingly by [[ambulances|ambulance]] to the hospital.

“He was disoriented but cooperative and seemed to recognize he may be having some sort of behavioral issue,” Lovell said.

He said deputies were told Best damaged a couple of items in the store but didn’t hurt or attack anyone; the store employees’ concern from was that he get the help he needed.

Lovell said he could not speak to what led to Best being released from the hospital’s care.

“The need to get some medical help, medical professionals and [[mental health]] professionals online, to deal with that outweighed any kind of potential criminal charges we had going on,” Lovell said.

Best killed his wife less than 12 hours after being taken to the hospital.

On Friday, EIRMC Director of Marketing [[Coleen Niemann]] told the [[Post Register]] that due to the [[Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act]] concerns, she could only confirm that Best was discharged from the [[hospitals|hospital]] on Thursday. Niemann did not answer questions as to who authorized Best’s release, referring back to her original statement.
