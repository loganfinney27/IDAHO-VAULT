As a contentious and costly [[election]] year begins, [[ORGANIZATIONS/PUBLICATIONS/Idaho Education News]] will follow the dollars.

We plan to write regularly on fundraising and spending, as all 105 legislative seats are on the ballot. Here’s a top 10 list: the incumbents and candidates who have raised the most money so far:

1. **[[Ted Hill]], R-[[Eagle]]: $110,310**. There’s an asterisk here: Hill, a first-term lawmaker, has loaned an eye-popping $106,860 to his campaign. Hill sits on the [[House Education]] Committee, and he has sponsored a bill to allow [[teachers]] and school staff to carry guns on campus; the [[Idaho House of Representatives|House]] passed this bill Wednesday.
2. **[[Jim Woodward]], [[Idaho Republican Party|Republican]], [[Sagle]]: $85,568**. A big-money rematch will unfold in [[State of Idaho|Idaho]]’s [[Legislative District 1|northernmost legislative district]]. Woodward, a former [[Senate member|state senator]], has raised much of his money from mainstream GOP circles — such as [[Idaho Power]], [[Spokane]], Wash.-based [[Clearwater Paper]], and [[State Board of Education]] member [[Shawn Keough]], his predecessor in the [[Idaho Senate|Senate]]. Woodward also has loaned his campaign $20,000. (More on his opponent later in this piece.)
3. **[[Steve Berch]], D-[[Boise]]: $75,764**. Berch, a three-term incumbent and [[House Education]] Committee member, represents West [[Boise]]’s battleground [[Legislative District 15|District 15]]. No opponent appears to be doing any fundraising so far.
4. **[[Brett Skidmore]], [[Idaho Republican Party|Republican]], [[Ammon]]: $64,700**. Skidmore is raising money from conservative circles — including [[Idaho Freedom Foundation]] vice chair [[Bryan Smith]] and former [[Lieutenant Governor|Lt. Gov.]] [[Janice McGeachin]]. Skidmore is taking on first-term [[House member|Rep.]] [[Josh Wheeler|Joshua Wheeler]], R-[[Ammon]], who has raised $27,279.
5. **[[Brandon Shippy]], [[Idaho Republican Party|Republican]], [[New Plymouth]]: $57,574**. Shippy’s donors include [[Idaho Freedom Foundation|Freedom Foundation]] chair [[Brent Regan]] and [[Idaho Freedom Foundation|Freedom Foundation]] board member [[Doyle Beck]]. Shippy is opposing Assistant Majority Leader [[Abby Lee]], R-[[Fruitland]], who has raised $10,150.
6. **[[Josh Tanner]], R-[[Eagle]]: $56,750**. Tanner, a first-term lawmaker and [[Joint Finance-Appropriations Committee]] member, has loaned $50,000 to his campaign.
7. **[[Scott Herndon]], R-[[Sagle]]: $56,602**. Herndon defeated Woodward in the 2022 GOP [[primary election|primary]], and now sits on the [[Senate Education]] Committee and [[Joint Finance-Appropriations Committee|JFAC]]. His donor base includes several [[Idaho Freedom Foundation|Freedom Foundation]] connections: Regan, Beck and Smith’s [[Idaho Falls]] law firm.
8. **[[Ben Toews]], R-[[Coeur d'Alene|Coeur d’Alene]]: $43,539**. The [[Senate Education]] vice chair has loaned $40,000 to his campaign.
9. **[[C. Scott Grow]], R-[[Eagle]]: $43,170**. Grow is [[Senate Finance|JFAC]]’s [[Idaho Senate|Senate]] co-chair. His donor base includes several industry [[political action committee|PACs]] and [[Idaho Chooses Life]].
10. **[[Codi Galloway]], [[Idaho Republican Party|Republican]], [[Boise]]: $42,980**. The former legislator has secured donations from at least four sitting [[Idaho Legislature|lawmakers]], and has loaned $20,000 to her campaign. She is positioning for a rematch in District 15. Democrat [[Rick Just]], who narrowly defeated Galloway in 2022, has raised $40,155.
