Ada County’s jail won’t be expanding in the near future.

The $49 million bond failed to get the necessary two-thirds of voters’ approval to pass, but it was close. With all precincts reporting, 65.8% voted in favor — just shy of the needed 66.7% — while 34.2% of voters were opposed.

Voter turnout was 35.9%.

The bond would have paid for 294 beds, an expanded kitchen, a laundry facility, and a warehouse for the Ada County Jail on Barrister Drive on the Boise Bench. It would have cost the average homeowner in Ada County roughly $13 on their property tax bill each year for the next twenty years.

All results for the measure are considered preliminary until they are certified by the Ada County Commissioners.

Commissioners could opt to refloat the bond at a future election or ask voters to fund the expansion via a lease agreement, which would only need a simple majority to pass.
