MOSCOW, Idaho — The [Latah County Jail](https://www.krem.com/article/news/local/latah-county-jail-future/293-d7cc72e5-0e92-433a-978d-1777cdda4b6d) is transitioning to a court holding and booking facility following the assessment of the jail, according to the Latah County Sheriff's Office.

The Sheriff's Office said the facility's fire suppression, plumbing, electrical and camera systems all need to be replaced to safely house inmates for over 24 hours.

“The expense of fixing these things is so immense that it's just as well that I start working on building a new facility," said Latah County Sheriff Richard Skiles.

The sheriff's office said the estimated cost for the repairs is $13 million. They added that the repairs would extend the life of the jail by five to 10 years before becoming obsolete.

“This situation is to the point where the liability for me is something that I have to take into consideration…" said Sheriff Skiles. "Just recently, I had to pull a couple of inmates out of a cell because of a leak, because of water breakage, pipes breaking.”

The sheriff's office said the current jail cannot meet basic requirements for continued certification, adding that the cost of retrofitting the current facility would roughly cost the same as building a new facility.

In a press release, the sheriff's office noted the transition to a court-holding facility will start no later than October 1, 2025, and will not impact employment within the sheriff's office. LCSO added that the jail will be staffed during normal court hours and will book and hold inmates during the day.

After hours, suspects who are arrested will be taken to the Nez Perce County Jail under a pending contract to house Latah County detainees, pending arraignment or posting bond, according to the sheriff's office.

The sheriff's office also said that detainees who don't post bond will stay at the Nez Perce County Jail until further order of the court.

The Sheriff said the Latah County Jail houses 23 inmates on average, daily. Combined, Latah County deputies and Moscow police make roughly 500 arrests yearly.

"The Latah County Sheriff's Office has been working with the Latah County Commissioners and the City of Moscow to develop a long-term plan that ensures a smooth and efficient transition," the sheriff's office said in the press release. "We are working closely with the Latah County Commissioners to explore various financial opportunities to fund a new jail facility."

The Latah County Jail was built and constructed in 1972.

The City of Moscow released a statement that said:

*The City of Moscow is aware that Latah County has decided to close the Latah County Jail by the end of September. We have been engaged in* [*conversations with the County on this topic over the last 10 months*](https://www.krem.com/article/news/local/moscow-city-council-discussions-potential-latah-county-jail-closure/293-4cfecf8f-7fb4-4525-96a8-4fc94880cf15) *due to the significant impact this action will have on our community and the Moscow Police Department. While the operation of the Jail is the responsibility of Latah County, the City of Moscow offered to share costs on the most immediate life safety repairs identified in July of last year while the County worked toward a long-term solution. However, the County felt the jail facility was beyond repair. It is our understanding that Latah County has entered into an agreement with Nez Perce County to house those in custody, and the City will now be responsible for transporting the nearly 250 individuals whom the Moscow Police Department arrests each year to the Nez Perce County Detention Center.*

*To reduce the impact on the City, we have worked with the County to develop a Memorandum of Agreement under which the Latah County Sheriff’s office has agreed to accept, process, and transport arrestees during the hours of operation of their court holding facility and on special event weekends in the City where arrests may be more frequent. The closure of the Latah County Jail will place significant financial and personnel stress upon the Moscow Police Department at a time when it is struggling with staffing, as are many law enforcement agencies across the nation. Additionally, long transports place both officers and arrestees at greater risk of vehicle accidents, medical emergencies, and increased transport costs, in addition to decreased coverage within city limits during the transport and return. The City of Moscow is committed to working cooperatively with Latah County to minimize the impact on the Moscow Police Department, the community, and Latah County. The City is hopeful that the County will move quickly toward a solution to address this critical community need."*
