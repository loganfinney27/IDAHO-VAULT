KOOTENAI COUNTY, ID. — Election Day is eight days away, and voters are already starting to vote early.

During early voting in Kootenai County, there's already been trouble at the elections office.

Police were called at least twice last week. One of the instances included a car crash in the parking lot.

It's why election officials are making some changes to where people can park, if they'd like to pass out fliers or hold up signs.

"It felt reckless," Christa Hazel, who lives in Kootenai County, said.

Early in the morning on Wednesday, October 25, Hazel got a call from her friend that was volunteering outside the Kootenai County Elections Office.

Their car had just been hit by another person, hoping to use that spot for electioneering.

"I've been involved in Kootenai County politics for close to 30 years," Hazel said. "I've never seen anything like this."

The Kootenai County Elections Office requires anyone wanting to hand out election fliers or put up signs to do so at least 100 feet away.

The county clerk says she believes it's what caused Wednesday's collision.

"The first three spots were past the 100 feet mark, and they were parking there and that was what caused that incident," Jennifer Locke said.

After that incident, Locke says the elections office met with its legal team, as well as county commissioners, to figure out a plan.

"We ended up putting up some signs from our building and grounds manager in the parking lot of the county (saying) that if you're not doing county business, you need to be doing county business or coming here to vote to park in these spots," Locke said.

Locke asks that people respect voters and don't block them from exercising their right to vote.

Since Wednesday, October 25, things have calmed down at the elections office, which Locke credits to their recent changes.
