A bigger health science campus is coming to [[Meridian]].

On Wednesday, the [[Meridian]] [[City Council]] approved an ordinance officially annexing and rezoning over 23 acres of land for [[Idaho State University]] to begin an expansion of its Meridian health science campus. The land was purchased by ISU in 2019, according to Gabe Bargen, the executive director of ISU Health Science Center.

The expansion could make ISU the closest thing Idaho has to a medical school, Bargen said.

“It’s always been part of the city’s vision or comprehensive plan to have a med tech corridor, and this just solidifies that,” Meridian Planning Supervisor Bill Parsons said. “It’s going to provide those educational opportunities to residents in Meridian to build upon what we have envisioned for the area.”

The land, according to Vice President of Campus Operations at ISU Brian Sagendorf, is just north of the campus’s existing facility on 1311 E Central Drive in Meridian. Around 1,000 students are enrolled and attending classes at the Meridian campus, which opened in 2009, according to Bargen.

The campus offers several programs in the health science field, including counseling, physical therapy, occupational therapy, speech language pathology, medical lab sciences, nursing and physician assistant programs, Bargen said.

Idaho State University is based in Pocatello and, along with Meridian, has satellite campuses in Idaho Falls and Twin Falls.

“We are so excited to have the Meridian campus become the hub and center for Idaho health sciences education for our state,” Sagendorf said. “We have such a need in the state of Idaho for additional health professionals, whether that’s physician assistants, nurses, occupational therapists, pharmacists, you name it, health professions are hot jobs for the state.”

The campus expansion is important because it will help bring those programs closer to people in Idaho, hopefully bolstering the health science careers in Idaho, Sagendorf said.

“This 23 acres will be a tremendous opportunity for us to have expanded classroom settings, expanded programs there, in a place where there are more and more health care partners,” Sagendorf said.

For the health science students who attend ISU’s Meridian campus, partnerships with local hospitals are crucial to get clinical rotations and experiences needed to be prepared to enter the field, which is why the campus is located so close to St. Luke’s Meridian Medical Center, just off of Eagle Road, Sagendorf said.

“Like any community, our goal is to have economic vitality, to provide and create jobs,” Parsons said. “If we have those educational components as part of our plan and people can stay here and go to school here, we hope that that will lead to jobs that they can find jobs that are currently lacking in our area.”

The annexation is just the first step in the process of the campus expansion, Parsons said. ISU will eventually have to come back to the city council and go through a plan unit development process, where they’ll provide the city with details of the layout of the campus.

Funding for the expansion is expected to continue coming in the next several years, but the first $5.3 million came from an appropriation from the state so ISU can begin infrastructure development for the expansion, Sagendorf said. The majority of funding will likely come from partnerships with other health care entities and partners.

The first steps to the expansion will be removing an old farmhouse and barn on the property, Sagendorf said, which could happen as soon as next summer. Plans for student housing and health care clinics on the property could come by 2030, Sagendorf said. The expansion, which will also include building research laboratories and classroom buildings, could take several decades to complete, Sagendorf said.

“It’s a very long-term expansion that will be done step by step,” Sagendorf said.
