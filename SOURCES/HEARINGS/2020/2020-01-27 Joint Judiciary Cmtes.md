Joint [[House Judiciary, Rules and Administration]] & [[Senate Judiciary and Rules]] presentation from IDOC

“Public Safety Through Individual Success”

[[Josh Tewalt]], Director of the Idaho [[Department of Correction]]

Overview

9,473 incarcerated in 9 state prisons, 1 in state private prison, 4 CRCs, 33 county jails, Texas

Total sentence = fixed term (minimum length) + indeterminant term (parole)

Held in County -> admitted to IDOC à Incarceration -> Release -> Community supervision

Newly sentenced (termers), returned violators, or retained jurisdiction (riders)

Incarceration period: Assessment -> Classification -> Programming -> Reentry

IDOC Goals: reduce crime, improve success for our staff and people in our care, reduce spending, increase confidence in IDOC

Balancing Accountability & Intervention

Accountability: show that actions aren’t acceptable (incarceration)

Intervention: provide opportunities and help where needed

IDOC staff 10x more likely than community to experience PTSD, lifespan shorter than community average

Current Challenges

Revolving Door

Unprecedented prison growth

Aging [[infrastructure]] not meeting needs

3/4 people entering IDOC prisons for new term commitments have failed on community supervision (not recidivism)

Prison growth -> sending people out of state (around 7,750 IDOC-only beds)

“Out of state placement is not a plan, it’s a mechanism to buy time.”

Over 1100 people in county jails

New contract Kitt Carson in Burlington, CO, 1,100 beds, consolidating under one roof

Had placement there several years ago

All of IDOC core programming will be available there,

TX contract about 20% are afforded a job, in CO will be 75%

Aging [[infrastructure]]; maintenance, alterations & repairs. [[Office of Performance Evaluations]] report tomorrow

$218M spent on prisons, $38M spent on in-community support (6x difference)

Plan to address challenges: Improve Community Outcomes, Increase Bed Capacity

Connection and Intervention Stations – lots of factors contribute to someone committing a crime. Parole Officers don’t have the resources they need to help people

Dosage parole pilot project: evaluating probation based on interventions, not duration of time

Creating reentry savings accounts (pending legislation this session)

Transitional housing partnerships

Increasing capacity: 160 CRC beds in Twin Falls (Oct 2020), 150 men’s prison beds (July 2020), 500 additional out-of-state beds (FY 2021), 150 Beds St Anthony Work Camp (FY 22), 130 Community Reentry Center (CRC) beds in [[North Idaho]]  
New capacity to realign with population needs later years

Ways for legislators to be involved

Visit IDOC facilities, also talk to people who’ve gone through the system, work in reentry, etc

Jury is still out on impact of Medicaid Expansion, but hopeful

[[Idaho Correctional Industries]] – self funded, depending on leadership can be totally bottom-line profit driven or can be focused on inmates, opportunities & skills once folks are out of custody
