**Mentor Judge Program** – Judge [[David Epis]], [[4th Judicial District]] ([[Elmore County]], 22 yrs)

[[Idaho Legislature|Legislature]] funds & supports these programs.

In past, very few programs for mentor judges, maybe a judge in the courthouse or next county, one statewide. Pilot program, today we have [[Judicial Performance Evaluations]]

Feb 2017 first group with new Evals

157 JPEs, discussions with facilitator judges, 35 new judges assigned mentors, every judge in the state has a local mentor judge today.

·       Local Mentor Judge – assigned immediately when you become a judge (next door or next county), feedback has been all positive.

·       Statewide Mentor Judge – one magistrate to about 10 w/ more trainees. Assigned immediately, further distance for a different perspective, lots of experience. Specific trainings, peer to peer, lots of practices borrowed from the corporate world. 6-month schedule. Take one day to follow new judge’s day, observe and give feedback.

·       Facilitator Judge – specifically for judges who have had a formal JPE. Assigned by supreme court, gets feedback from attorneys, court staff, etc. Don’t need a lot of prompting, want to discuss areas they need to work on.

JPEs every 3 years

Updating judges, getting feedback from them on the programs this year

recognition from [[National Judicial College]] in Reno, will have an ID member on their advisory board

**Education for New Judges** – Judge [[Annie McDevitt]], [[4th Judicial District]] ([[Ada County]], 2 yrs)

McDevitt experience – local & statewide mentors.

Over past 5 years: 67 new judges, 25 district judges, 42 magistrates. Over past 5 years 47% of judiciary is new judges

8-day orientations (two 4-day sessions, fall & spring), Instructed by all levels of judges, contact info of experienced judges. ~15 trainees

Nat’l Judicial College (Reno, NV) general jurisdiction training. ~150 trainees

Annual conferences: Magistrate, District. Annual Judges conference (Mag & Dist together)

Multi-disciplinary conferences, attended by judges & others: Drug & Mental Health Courts, Children & Families (the two rotate year to year). Standalone Domestic Violence conference when funds are available.

Paperless [[Odyssey system]] trainings, nat’l conferences, Judicial Performance Evaluations

**Challenges on Civil Commitments** – Judge [[John Lothspeich]], [[5th Judicial District]] ([[Jerome County]] – 1 year as judge, longer experience as private practice & prosecutor)

Mental [[civil commitments]] occur without hearings. Emergency responders call judge to come to [[emergency room]], obtain order w/in 24 hours. First & second designated examiners. Severe shortage statewide of designated examiners (1st DE master’s in social work, 2nd DE psychiatrists).

Organic Brain Disorder or Organic Biochemical Disorder

Commitment can last up to 1 year

[[jails]] can’t be used, even temporarily

Patients must be placed in 24 hours, often doesn’t happen bc of lack of beds

Large increase of commitments over last 5 years. 18% increase statewide, some districts >50%

Patients often released w/ no treatment plan bc need for beds

45% of [[suicide]] victims in the state had seen their [[primary care physician]] in the previous 30 days

Vast majority of Lothspeich’s instances of civil commitments have been for attempted suicide

Assisted outpatient treatments – hospital/facility submit reports to the court

Could save money for state & counties by avoiding recommitment
