[[House Transportation and Defense]], Jan 14, 1:30pm

Chair - [[Joe Palmer]]

[[Budget Stabilization Fund]] (H 309) – Rep. Palmer presenting

Endowment fund, last session bill passed House, ran out of time in Senate

Multiple members, [[Legislative Services Office|LSO]] staff, [[lobbyists]] have crafted it

[[Rainy Day Fund]] -> interest earning account, interest -> endowment fund for transportation

EXACT SAME BILL as last year w/ updated fund amounts and dates

This year money will be moved into account to earn interest, uses of money will be determined next year.

[[Clark Kauffman]]—concern about one [[highway districts|highway district]] being excluded from grant process. Response: different entity than other highway districts. They have built-in employees for services that others don’t. [[Local Highway Technical Assistance Council]] specifically came forward and asked to do it this way.

[[Steven Harris]]—how much money is generated in a typical year? Last year, would have generated 17 million in interest. Investing taxpayer money to generate funds to use. Nearly half a billion sitting right now generating no interest. Rainy day funds were spent down in recession, this can create a self-refilling fund (my words, not Palmer) for [[transportation]].

[[John Gannon]]—what will the fund be invested in? 6.25% interest rate? Risk to principal? Same investment as the [[Millennium Fund]], averages 3-4% return.

[[Keith Bybee]], Deputy BPA LSO – refer to page 1 lines 27-28. Directs [[State Treasurer]] to invest public funds in same way as Millennium Fund.

Gannon response – rainy day fund doesn’t get used until downturn. What happened to the Mil Fund during the recession? Only year that MilF lost market value was in 2009 – 16.1%. Following years made up 8% and 24%.

[[Melissa Wintrow]] – will you run through the mechanics? Basics: 272 million from [[Budget Stabilization Fund]] (currently 373 mil) leaves 101 mill remaining. Move 272 million to endowment-type fund ([[Endowment Reserve Investment Fund]]) that will earn higher interest than idle monies. Create stable interest stream to fund transportation [[Local Strategic Initiatives Program]] (currently 5%).

Wintrow—Millennium has a board, how is this one distributed? Statute drives appropriation here. Split 60-40 between ITD & local

Gannon—annual interest from fund this will be invested in? “Idle Pool” tied to prime interest rates (monetary policy, the [[Federal Reserve]]). Current interest income from that is transferred to [[Permanent Building Fund]]

Moving monies into this new fund would allow treasurer to invest more aggressively.

Motion: [[Gayann DeMordaunt]] moves to approve & send to floor with do pass. Unanimous pass by voice.

**Transportation, Funding, Sales Tax (RS) – Palmer**

Bill repeated from last year as well. Changes 1% to 2% in [[sales tax distribution formula]] to [[transportation]], changes 18 million in funding last year to 36 million this year. 100 million increase in sales tax revenue last year, so would move 18 mil more of that into transportation.

[[sales tax]] is charged every time a car is sold, tires, etc. Everything is moved on trucks, etc. A huge amount of money comes from sales tax, so we should use a portion of that to fund [[roads]].

Gannon—[[Change in Employee Compensation|CEC]] committee keeps hearing the budget is super tight, state employees only get 2% raise, etc. How to justify this expenditure when we’re being told to cut budgets elsewhere? If we invest in transportation system that will bring in more sales tax moving forward.

Harris—approx estimate of sales tax from auto related items? Off top of Palmer’s head, 150mil comes from new car sales.

Harris moves to introduce RS. Unanimous pass by voice.

**Veteran Definition (RS)** -- [[Marv Hagedorn]] for [[Division of Veterans Services]] agency bill

Cleans up veteran definition started many years ago. Currently define veteran in Title 65 Ch 203. Another section has an old definition. This strikes old definition and creates references to existing.

[[Scott Syme]]—move to introduce RS. Unanimous pass by voice.
