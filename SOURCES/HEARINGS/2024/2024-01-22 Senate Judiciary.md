**[[(2024) Senate Bill 1232|SB 1232]] - [[Senate member|Sen.]] [[Scott Herndon]] - [[parental rights]] in [[child protection]] cases**

"Now I knew a lot as a parent already at that time what my relationship would be within a CPS investigation." Herndon said. 

"Here's your rights, but you want to cooperate to the extend that where there's a legitimate concern, you need to make sure that whoever's investigating is satisfied with the legitimate concern." 

"It basically creates a notification requirement that in a CPS investigation where parents are being investigated by the Department of Health and Welfare... They're going to receive some written notification of what their rights are." 

Herndon: trifold pamphlet currently published by IDHW would satisfy requirements in the bill - started under Director [[Dave Jeppesen]] after Herndon came to IDHW with concerns as a private citizen 

This year's version doesn't have civil right to sue like last year, and failure to notify in writing does not jeopardize base investigation 

"The department is doing this now, and they tell me they're giving one of these out in every investigation, but the department could always change its mind... I think what we accomplish in passing this legislation is balancing the state's compelling interests without our parents in the state feeling run over."

8,600 investigations where CPS workers came to people's doors out of 24,000 calls and referrals

Herndon is not opposed in theory to adding language 

[[Melissa Wintrow]] questions - child's rights to safety - process 
This notification would be required upon first *in-person* direct contact. 
"That's the time I think when the parents are going to be the most overwhelmed, especially if law enforcement is there," Herndon said.
