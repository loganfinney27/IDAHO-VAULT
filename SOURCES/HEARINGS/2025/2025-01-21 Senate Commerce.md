[[Mark Harris]] [[RS]] 31866 re: [[counselors]] and [[therapists]]

Last year HB 490 re: [[Board of Professional Counselors and Marriage and Family Therapists|licensing]] fingerprint and background checks for registration - relied on interstate [[Counseling Compact]] which did not pass - this RS would amend licensing requirements for fingerprint and background to only when [[Division of Occupational and Professional Licenses|DOPL]] finds it necessary 

[[Treg Bernt]] moves to print, [[Jim Guthrie]] seconds, unanimous voice vote


SB 1006 [[Todd Lakey]] - [[construction]] building projects inside the [[State of Idaho|state]] is subject to only ID laws, (e.g. not subject to [[California]] or [[New York]] building requirements or litigation in their courts) [[building codes]]

"A backstop that allows a contractor here in Idaho to say, no, we are only subject here to Idaho law." 

[[Jim Guthrie]] question: how would that apply if it went up to [[district court]] or [[U.S. Supreme Court|SCOTUS]]? Probably not any states' laws that would supercede ours?
Lakey: If located in a certain jurisdiction they should be subject to that one

[[Building Contractors Association of Southwest Idaho]] testimony in favor 

[[Tammy Nichols]] moves do pass, [[Janie Ward-Engelking]] seconds

[[James Ruchti]] supports, would even like to expand it to other contracts, but sees why [[construction]] is a priority. These provisions address situations with parties that wrote the contract to force litigation to happen in a friendly or favorable jurisdiction, adding burden to Idaho residents

adhesion provisions in contracts require arbitration somewhere they want to use, even though [[Idaho Constitution]] provides us right to [[trial by jury]] 

SB 1006 passes committee unanimously 

[[administrative rules]] review, Justin Touchstone with [[Division of Occupational and Professional Licenses|DOPL]]
