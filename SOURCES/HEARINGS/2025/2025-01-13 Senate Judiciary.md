[[Senate Judiciary and Rules]]

January 13, 1:30 PM

First meeting

APPOINTMENT: Kirk Nelson of [[Blackfoot]] to the Sexual Offender Management Board

32-year career in [[law enforcement]] with Pocatello [[police]] until 2007

Polygraph business, largely with sex offender and drug courts

Hadn’t been interested in serving on the board in the past due to Boise travel but remote meetings and Zoom make it more accessible

WINTROW question re: new appointment, what do you bring to the role

“I pride myself on being neutral,” he said. “A person does not do well in the criminal justice system… if they have skeletons in their closet, if you will, that they haven’t disclosed.”

LAKEY question: how often do registered sex offenders have to do polygraph

Usually twice a year, every 6 months, for a “maintenance test” on how they’re managing

One of the hot button issues facing the field is whether polygraph testing of juveniles is best practice. He has the contract with Dept of Juvenile Corrections and St Anthony, eastern ID

Vote to come at the next Senate Judiciary meeting.

PAGE introduction: Anna Calahan of Nampa

Must cover equivalent to VA burial benefit cost of $978 for plot, $400 or 250 for marker

Rerun of rule change from last year that was not concurred due to administrative error

RS 31860: anti-SLAPP

Sen. Brian Lenney

“This is a bill that’s about protecting free speech in Idaho by discouraging SLAPP lawsuits.”

Idaho is one of fifteen states with no anti-SLAPP protections

Expedites process for handling frivolous lawsuits

Puts suit on hold when someone files an anti-SLAPP motion

Sets clear rules for quickly dismissing baseless suits – moves process to front end

Allows winning side to recover costs including attorney’s fees

Deters litigation tourism

Includes automatic penalties for SLAPP filers

“Good people don’t deserve to get buried for exercising their First Amendment rights.”

Foreman moves to print, Ruchti seconds, unanimous voice vote
