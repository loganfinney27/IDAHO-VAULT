[[RS]] 32055 – Search authority – [[John Shirts]] 

When granted probation and parole, sign a waiver of search rights

Parole officers are understaffed and overworked, inconsistent monitoring and compliance

Following other states’ example – extends search authority to all [[law enforcement]] officers

GARNER: Where are they required to waive rights? SHIRTS: In administrative rule right now.

HILL: This seems simple, is there any pushback? SHIRTS: We are following other states.

Constitutionality of waiving Fourth Amendment rights? OK as voluntary condition of parole.

Expanding officer authority, potential for harassment? Language in bill based on case law.

SCOTT: Broad, electronic devices. Who has this authority and how is it expanding? SHIRTS: Currently probation and parole officers have this search authority. Expanded to all officers.

EHARDT: Would this stop someone getting out? SHIRTS: No just parole to get out early.

GANNON: Probation piece of this? SHIRTS: Intended to cover just supervised probation.

RETURNED TO SPONSOR

[[RS]] 32035 – Riots – [[Jaron Crane]]

Brought by [[Nampa]] [[police]] department detective. Riot definition includes physical injury to any person, damage to property, disturbance to public peace. Penalties are attached to the second two but not physical injury to a person. I would consider this a cleanup bill, he says.

[[RS]] 32154 – [[Artificial intelligence]] – [[John Gannon]]

[[artificial intelligence|AI]] can reproduce identifiable people’s voices. Recent article on surge of AI voice scams during tax season. Consumer protection, you must disclose clearly and conspicuously that communication is artificial intelligence. Applies when one would reasonably believe they are speaking to a human. Exemption for basic business operations like a call directory. Private right of action with damages for time to scam by misleading AI. [[Attorney General|AG]] actions too.

HB 37 – Methods of [[execution]] ([[firing squad]]) – Skaug

Skaug believes firing squad is more reliable, less susceptible to [[U.S. Constitution|constitutional]] challenge than [[lethal injection]] 

[[Dianna David]], [[Survivors Empowered]], unclear where difference in funding will come from re: firing squad chamber. Bill would make [[State of Idaho|Idaho]] only state with primary method of firing squad. Concerns for staff.

“It’s brutal, it’s gruesome. It’s violent, on the most evil level you can imagine.”

[[Daniel Murphy]], anti-death penalty supporters have eliminated methods one by one, firing squad is “cheap and fast” and might be most humane and painless of the available options

[[David Powell]], [[Death Penalty Action]], opposition to bill and to the death penalty. Urges creation of a subcommittee to study the cost and benefits. Psychological toll on officers and victim families. Expenses.

Kylie Castella (?), agree with question about costs for construction, cost for mental health leave for staff that carry out execution. Agree with intent to be humane. Worry that as the political climate gets more heated, opens door to execution with guns under martial law.

[[LaMont Anderson]], [[Deputy Attorney General]]. Firing squad has been held constitutional, specific [[judges]] have opined it may be better as more humane and faster. Biggest advantage is actually less litigation. PIZZUTO’s conviction was affirmed in 2002. We have been litigating nothing but the method of execution for over 20 years. CREECH’s 2023.  
“Bullets aren’t going to interact with medication. We’re not going to have that problem.”

EHARDT: Mental health of firing squad is a concern, how would the members be chosen? ANDERSON: My understanding is Tewalt has not completed execution protocol, but it’s first volunteers that participate in any execution. Could do automatic protocol with one button.

[[Anthony Oxsen]], Nampa, supports bill, see on news all the time that lethal injections aren’t working. Firing squad has been described as barbaric, but it works 100% of the time. Their penalty is death, it’s up to us to carry that out.

Kate Lopez, [[Twin Falls]], considers bill “barbarism of the mind and the body” hood over the head, restraints on limbs, targets on body. Just because it’s legal doesn’t mean it’s right. These actions will weigh on employees asked to carry out this duty. Who wants to visit and spend tourist dollars in a [[State of Idaho|state]] with wild west justice?

SKAUG CLOSING: Up to [[Department of Correction|IDOC]] director [[Josh Tewalt]] to write the protocol, he has said it will be mechanized. Primary goal is that it is dignified and humane for all involved including the convicted. There will not be a request for more funds, those are already included in [[Department of Correction|IDOC]] budget. There are 144 executions by firing squad in our [[United States of America|nation]]’s history.

EHARDT moves do pass. No questions. SKAUG requests roll call vote.

GANNON: Cost will be over 1 million. [[South Carolina]] had a lethal injection last week.
