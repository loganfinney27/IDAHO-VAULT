RS 31831 [[wildfire|Wildfire]] Insurance - [[Lance Clow]] 

[[Dean Cameron]], Director of [[Department of Insurance]] 

"This bill, this idea, has been under consideration by the Department of Insurance for several years," Clow said. "We thought it was at least appropriate to bring the topic up today." 

"We started working on this a couple years ago when we started seeing the insurance market tightening up," Cameron said. "Some carriers were still here in the state but tightening who they would accept." 

"Many of the issues could have been prevented or mitigated. So what the bill does is it takes two proven insurance strategies," Cameron said. 

Creates a pool and two revenue sources that wouldn't affect general fund 

Creates a board: Appointed + Insurance director + State fire marshal 

Grants and helping homeowners with home hardening strategies 

Southeastern states have used this model for hurricanes and it has worked 

[[Kyle Harris]]: walk us through some of these details 

Cameron: some of this requires board to use actuarial principles and would allow them to contract out with the funds they receive 

1.5% premium tax exists today. Some goes to firefighters retirement fund and some to high risk fund. If we have future growth in premium tax, one fourth of that amount would be diverted to this new insurance pool 

Some small amount of cigarette tax money goes to fire marshal but they have no way to use it, so divert to this pool rather than revert. "It just sort of sits there." Have considered asking for repeal but haven't gotten there. 

Stamping fee for carriers not admitted in the state but who offer insurance lines that no admitted carriers offer. [[Surplus Lines Association of Idaho]] 

Also includes ability to charge assessment if board and industry overspend 

Berch: during hearing would like information on how diverting funding streams would impact all of those prior sources; total dollar amounts; prospects for the future i.e. growth; grant decisions and means testing 

Berch: Are the insurance industry folks citing climate change in this? Cameron: There are multiple sources causing tightening in the market. Forest fires are at the top of the list. Increased costs for insurance carriers' own insurance. More expensive to insure houses with higher valuations. 

Monks moves to introduce. Divided voice vote (no from [[Kyle Harris]] & [[Kent Marmon]] & [[Cornel Rasor]]
