The [[Legislative District 32]] Committee voted to censure [[State of Idaho|state]] [[House member|Rep.]] [[Stephanie Mickelsen]], issuing a vote no confidence in her on Tuesday. It also released a “resolution of guidance” for [[House member|Rep.]] [[Wendy Horman]] and [[Senate member|Sen.]] [[Kevin Cook]].

![Resolution of Censure for Rep. Stephanie Mickelsen - 12-12-2023](https://bloximages.chicago2.vip.townnews.com/postregister.com/content/tncms/assets/v3/editorial/f/48/f48f6c36-9bb6-11ee-8d95-d33150721f30/657d0518bdb6b.preview.jpg)
*GOP [[Legislative District 32]] [[central committees|Committee]]'s Resolution of Censure for [[House member|Rep.]] [[Stephanie Mickelsen]]*

[[Bonneville County Republican Central Committee|The committee]] determined by majority vote that Mickelsen had violated the [[Idaho Republican party]] platform on eight allegations — her votes for: “[[Idaho Launch]] grants ([[(2023) House Bill 24|H.B. 24]]), appropriating money to [[WWAMI joint medical education program]] (S.B. 1147) and [[budget|appropriating funds]] for [[state colleges|colleges and universities]] (S.B. 1176); and her votes against: prohibiting state government from paying membership fees or dues with public funds (H.B. 91), a bill to restrict access of minor to sexual exhibitions (H.B. 265), a [[property tax]] amendment (H.B. 273) and overriding [[Governor|Gov.]] [[Brad Little]]’s [[veto]] of the School and Library Protection Act ([[(2023) House Bill 314|H.B. 314]]).

![Resolution of Guidance for Rep. Wendy Horman - 12-12-2023](https://bloximages.chicago2.vip.townnews.com/postregister.com/content/tncms/assets/v3/editorial/1/f4/1f4500da-9bb7-11ee-82f3-f36687eb930c/657d0545a1af4.preview.jpg)
*GOP [[Legislative District 32]] [[central committees|Committee]]'s Resolution of Guidance for [[House member|Rep.]] [[Wendy Horman]]*

All three [[Idaho Legislature|legislators]] provided the [[Post Register]] with copies of the resolutions.

In an interview Friday, [[Legislative District 32]] [[central committees|Committee Chairman]] [[Doyle Beck]] described the committee’s final four-hour meeting held at his office Tuesday.

“It was thoughtful. It was majority rules. It’s a process. … It was merely an investigation or an inquiry,” he said. “It would have turned out substantially better for all of (the [[Idaho Legislature|legislators]]) had they chose to participate in this project.”

In an email to the [[Post Register]] on Friday, Cook said he would have participated if the committee’s votes and proceedings were open to the public.

![Resolution of Guidance for Senator Kevin Cook - 12-12-2023](https://bloximages.chicago2.vip.townnews.com/postregister.com/content/tncms/assets/v3/editorial/4/20/420c9f42-9bb7-11ee-a515-5ff8e67f36ef/657d0592baa5b.preview.jpg)
*GOP [[Legislative District 32]] Committee's Resolution of Guidance for [[Senate member|Senator]] [[Kevin Cook]]*

“I did not refuse to appear before the jury,” he wrote. “From day one, I told Mr. Beck I would show up at his hearing if he would guarantee the trial and voting would be open to the public. We discussed this for several weeks. Then to my surprise, I received an email stating the date of my trial and an attached document with my 10 allegations. I am really disappointed in the leadership of the [[Idaho Republican Party|IDGOP]] and [[Bonneville County Republican Central Committee|BCRCC]]. Everything about our party should be transparent and open to the public.”

Mickelsen said she does not believe the [[Idaho Legislature|legislators]] should have taken part in the hearings.

“This was never about having a conversation,” Mickelsen said. “This was about having a tribunal. This was about having a witch hunt. It read like a court charging document that we had broken the law. … I've watched this group of people for many, many years. It's never 'Can we have a conversation?' It's always, 'How can we burn you down to the ground?'"

[[Bonneville County Republican Central Committee|Bonneville County GOP]] State Committeeman Anthony Tirino disagreed, saying elected officials should be held accountable to the local GOP [[precinct committeemen|precinct committee]] officers who submit petitions with allegations about their voting record.

“We always have had that right to voice disapproval in the decisions that our elected representatives make on our behalf,” Tirino said. “It is a representative [[U.S. Constitution|constitutional]] republic. They act on our behalf. We have the right to take them to task. We should be that involved where we call them to the carpet.”

Both [[Idaho Legislature|legislators]] and precinct committee officers are elected by the people, he said.

The committee consisted of 13 [[precinct committeemen|precinct committee officers]] or assistant precinct committee officers — including [[Kathryn Hitch]] (Precinct 21), [[Carolyn Harrison]] (Precinct 24), [[Kathy Kula]] (Precinct 25), [[Michael Colson]] (Precinct 26), [[Rusty Devereaux]] (Precinct 27), [[David Hay]] (Precinct 28), [[Nick Contos]] (Precinct 37), [[Lisa Keller]] (Precinct 38), [[Mark Bitter]] (Precinct 46), [[Chad Murdock]] (Precinct 48), [[Kirk Petersen]] (Precinct 50), [[Ben Baker]] (Precinct 51) and [[Brian Minner]] (District 52 APCO).

The committee thanked Horman for the written responses she provided and encouraged her to participate in “preliminary hearings before arguing about the process in the media” in the future.

For Cook, the committee determined he violated the [[Idaho Republican Party|Republican]] Party platform on four counts by voting for the “[[Idaho Launch]] Grant, WWAMI funding, college and university funding and against Freedom in Education Savings accounts.”

*[[Senate member|Senator]] [[Kevin Cook]]'s email to the [[Post Register]] - 12-15-2023*

In his e-mail to the [[Post Register]], Cook responded to each of the allegations.

On [[Idaho Launch]], he wrote, “I am a 100% believer in this. But, if it doesn't work as we plan there is a sunset date on this bill that will allow the program to die a natural death unless the [[Idaho Legislature|legislators]] act upon it.”

On S.B. 1038, the education savings accounts bill, he wrote, “The [[state of Idaho]] is supposed to give money to a private school and have no say or view into how that money is spent or what they teach. Really? The majority of the [[Idaho Senate|Republicans]] voted against this bill.”

On S.B. 1147 and 1176 he said the bills were “hashed out and resolved in [[Joint Finance-Appropriations Committee|JFAC]] and the opponents know it. [[Joint Finance-Appropriations Committee|JFAC]] received a letter from the [[University of Washington]] stating that no funds from the [[state of Idaho]] go to support any of the objectionable classes that were being discussed and listed in this allegation. The [[Bonneville County Republican Central Committee|BCRCC]] needs to do a better job at their homework.”

The committee's resolution provided Cook five points of guidance and asked him to "publicly reaffirm his commitment to the [[Idaho Republican Party Platform]] and to consider the Platform when evaluating proposed legislation."

Under the party’s new Republican Platform Enforcement rule, if [[Idaho Legislature|legislators]] who are censured are brought in for a second hearing for continued alleged violations of the Idaho GOP platform, they can be prohibited from running as a [[Idaho Republican Party|Republican]] for the next five years.

Quoting Idaho GOP Rules Article XX. Section 6, Beck clarified that a decision to remove the [[Idaho Republican Party|Republican]] label from an elected official would require 60 percent approval of committee members, whereas the decision to censure required 50 percent.

Since the committee did not censure Cook or Horman, the entire process would have to be repeated twice for them to be removed from the Republican Party.

In a statement Friday, Beck wrote, "The [[Legislative District 32]] [[Idaho Republican Party|Republicans]] believe questioning a [[Idaho Legislature|representative of the people]] is a serious matter. We also believe that it is important to promote true and helpful communication, as may be needed from time to time, between [[political parties]] and those who use the party name to get elected."

Mickelsen disagreed with the Legislative District 32 Committee’s approach.

“They're acting like a private political club, not a [[political parties|political party]] that's supposed to help promote and build the [[Idaho Republican Party|Republican]] brand and help [[U.S. Republican Party|Republican]] candidates campaign in the [[general election]],” Mickelsen said. “… I think that's a great disservice to our community, and they're really hurting the Republican brand in [[Bonneville County]] by their actions."
