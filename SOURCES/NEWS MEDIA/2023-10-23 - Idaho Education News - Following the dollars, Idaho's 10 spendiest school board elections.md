[[Kevin Richert]], [[ORGANIZATIONS/PUBLICATIONS/Idaho Education News]]

Most Idaho [[school board]] races are still low-key and low-budget elections.

However, there are a few exceptions.

And here are the 10 spendiest trustee races in Idaho, based on contributions reported as of Monday.

Idaho Education News will continue to track campaign finance reports through the Nov. 7 elections — and beyond.

### **[[West Ada School District|West Ada]], Zone 5: $58,845**

**[[Tom Moore]]**: $50,784, including $50,067 in loans from the candidate.

**[[René Ozuna]]** (incumbent): $8,061, including $1,000 from [[Meridian]] [[mayor]] [[Robert Simison]], and $1,000 from [[Engineered Structures Inc.]], a builder with ties to West Ada. 

### **[[Coeur d'Alene School District|Coeur d'Alene]], Zone 2: $22,692**

**[[James McAndrew]]**: $16,523, including $200 from former [[Lieutenant Governor|Lt. Gov.]] [[Jack Riggs]] and $50 from former [[Board of Trustees - NIC|North Idaho College trustee]] and state Sen. [[John Goedde]], R-Coeur d’Alene.

**[[Yasmin Harris]]**: $5,567.

**[[Paul Mahlow]]**: $602, entirely self-funded. 

### **[[Kuna Joint School District|Kuna]], Zone 1: $21,614**

**[[Hillary Lowe]]**: $14,159, including $1,000 apiece from [[Tyler Youngkin|Tyler]] and [[Constance Youngkin]], San Ysidro, Calif., and [[Quincy Youngkin]], Kuna; $1,000 from [[W. Scott Schirmer]], Phoenix; $1,000 from [[William Brownlee]], Peoria, Ariz.

**[[Joy Thomas]]** (incumbent): $7,455, largely from [[Idaho Democratic Party|Democratic]] donors, including former gubernatorial candidate and Boise school trustee [[AJ Balukoff]] ($1,000); [[Susie Balukoff]] ($1,000) and House Minority Leader [[Ilana Rubel]], D-Boise ($1,000).

### **[[Blaine County School District|Blaine County]], Zone 5: $20,244**

**[[Lara Stone]]** (incumbent): $10,804, including $200 from former House Minority Leader [[Wendy Jaquet]], D-Ketchum, and [[Jim Jaquet]].

**[[Bailey Brooks]]**: $9,440, including $1,000 from [[Push Back Idaho]].

### **Kuna, Zone 2: $17,454**

**[[Kristi Hardy]]:** $15,954, including $1,000 apiece from Tyler, Constance and Quincy Youngkin; Schirmer; and Brownlee.

**[[James Grant]]:** $1,500, entirely self-funded.

### **West Ada, Zone 4: $11,890**

**[[Miguel Deluna]]**: $6,165, including $1,000 from Moore.

**[[David Binetti]]** (incumbent): $5,000, including $4,000 from the candidate.

**[[Mari Gates]]**: $725.

### **Coeur d’Alene, Zone 3: $7,305**

**[[Heather Tenbrink]]** (incumbent): $5,295.

**[[Richard Blatt]]**: $2,010, including $100 from [[Brent Regan]], [[Kootenai County Republican Central Committee]] chairman and an [[Idaho Freedom Foundation]] board member.

### **[[Caldwell School District|Caldwell]], Zone 3: $7,283**

**[[Travis Manning]]** (incumbent): $6,625, including $1,000 from [[AJ Balukoff]]; $100 from former Boise school superintendent [[Don Coberly]]; and $100 from former state Rep. [[John McCrostie]], D-Garden City.

**[[Nicole Trakel]]**: $658, including $200 from state Sen. [[Cindy Carlson]], R-Riggins, and $50 from [[Kent Marmon]], Caldwell.

### **[[West Bonner County School District|West Bonner]], Zone 1: $6,050**

**[[Margaret Hall]]** (incumbent): $5,207.

**[[Alan Galloway]]**: $843.

### **[[Nampa School District|Nampa]], Zone 2: $5,936**

**[[Adam Schasel]]**: $5,122, including $100 from [[Reclaim Idaho]] co-founder [[Luke Mayville]].

**[[David Jennings]]**: $814, including $491.74 from Nampa trustee [[Brook Taylor]].
