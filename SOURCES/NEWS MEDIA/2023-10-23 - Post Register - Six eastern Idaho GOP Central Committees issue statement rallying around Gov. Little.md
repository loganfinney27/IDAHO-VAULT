The [[U.S. Republican Party|Republican]] [[Central Committees]] from six [[eastern Idaho]] [[counties]] issued a joint statement of support for [[Governor|Gov.]] [[Brad Little]] at a Multi-[[counties|County]] [[Idaho Republican Party|Republican]] Fall Banquet Saturday at [[Madison High School]] in [[Rexburg]].

The official statement expresses moderate [[Idaho Republican Party|Republicans]]’ opposition to the current direction of the [[Idaho Republican Party|state GOP party]].

“We reject the decision of the [[Idaho Republican Party State Central Committee|Idaho Republican Party State Central Committee]] to express a vote of ‘no confidence’ regarding [[Governor]] Little and 14 of our [[Idaho Republican Party|Republican]] [[State of Idaho|Idaho]] [[House member|legislators]],” the statement said. “Our [[Governor]] and our [[Senate member|Legislators]] receive their authority from the voters, not from our party, or any party.

“Each individual office holder has a moral and [[Idaho Constitution|constitutional]] obligation to make their own decisions about difficult and important issues. We do not need or want all [[Idaho Republican Party|Republicans]] to think the same. It is wrong for our party to reprimand our elected officials for voting as they conclude is best for [[State of Idaho|Idaho]] and their individual [[Legislative Districts|districts]].”

Little and [[Lieutenant Governor|Lt. Gov.]] [[Scott Bedke]] addressed the GOP representatives at the event.

[[Madison County Republican Central Committee|Madison]], [[Jefferson County Republican Central Committee|Jefferson]], [[Fremont County Republican Central Committee|Fremont]], [[Lemhi County Republican Central Committee|Lemhi]], [[Clark County Republican Central Committee|Clark]] and [[Butte County Republican Central Committee|Butte]] [[counties]]’ GOP [[Central Committees]] voted to sign on to the statement. The [[Teton County Republican Central Committee|Teton County GOP Central Committee]] also participated in the banquet, but did not sign the declaration.

“There’s some divided parts of the party right now, and the majority of this group is more supportive of a more moderate approach to governing,” said [[Randy Sutton]], [[Madison County Republican Central Committee|Madison County GOP Central Committee]] chairman.

The statement also expressed support for the [[Idaho Republican Women’s Federation]], [[College Republicans of Idaho|College Young Republicans]] and the [[Idaho Young Republicans]].

“These groups have provided much of the labor and organization that have made our party successful. We regret that the current leadership of the [[Idaho Republican Party]] has promoted and passed a rule change that revoked the vote of each group in the [[Idaho Republican Party State Central Committee|State Central Committee]],” the statement said.

In a speech at the event, Little stood by his record as [[Governor]].

“Obviously myself and all my good legislative partners, we have the distinction of offering the most tax relief per capita of any state in the [[United States of America|Union]],” Little said. “We were the first state to ban [[Critical Race Theory|CRT]] (Critical Race Theory). We were the first state to pass [[Fairness in Women’s Sports]]. We don’t disrespect parents, we respect parents. We don’t implement woke technology in all the different facets of government, particularly in schools. We always honor the [[Second Amendment]] and the sanctity of life.”

He touted the state’s AAA [[credit rating]], which was recently upgraded.

“Besides doing back-to-back-to-back-to-back tax decreases, we’ve made incredible investments in [[education]],” Little said. “We’ve made great investments in our [[infrastructure]]. We’ve made investments in [[cities|municipal]] water and [[agriculture|agricultural]] water — both below the ground and above the ground. … The amazing thing is we’re doing all of that while lowering taxes, without going into debt.”

Little was re-elected in 2022 with 358,598 votes, receiving support from more than 60% of voters and winning in 43 out of 44 counties, the statement said.

In its June 24 summer meeting in [[Challis]], the state GOP Central Committee, led by Chairwoman [[Dorothy Moon]], approved a vote of no confidence in Little and 14 Republican legislators for vetoing and failing to override the veto for [[(2023) House Bill 314|H.B. 314]]. 

H.B. 314 would have allowed individuals to file legal action against [[libraries]] for $2,500 for disseminating material deemed “harmful to minors.”

In his April 5 [[veto letter]], Little wrote, “This legislation makes sweeping, blanket assumptions on materials that could be determined as ‘harmful to minors’ in a local library, and it will force one interpretation of that phrase onto all the patrons of the library.”

He said the legislation would have created “a library bounty system that will only increase the costs local libraries incur, particularly rural libraries. These costs will be forced onto property taxpayers of Idaho or cause the libraries to close to minors altogether.”

The [[Post Register]] reached out to the Idaho GOP for an interview responding to the statement from the six counties’ GOP central committees, but did not receive a response prior to publication.

Several counties’ GOP officials expressed frustration with recent rule changes and tactics being employed by the state party, citing the resignation of former [[Idaho Republican Party|Idaho GOP]] [[Republican National Committee|National Committeeman]] [[Damond Watkins]] and the state GOP’s intervention in local [[Bingham County Republican Central Committee|Bingham County GOP Central Committee]] elections.

“They’re using strong-arm tactics, and it’s a bullying situation,” [[Butte County Republican Central Committee]] Chairwoman [[Kaylynn Smith]] said.

[[Marsha Bjornn]], [[Madison County Republican Central Committee|Madison County GOP Central Committee]] state committeewoman, agreed, “I see meanness, putting people down, trying to destroy people’s reputations. ... We should have a right to vote for who we want to, and if we are loyal to our party 75 or 80% of the time, that’s good enough. We don’t always have to choose just our party. We need to learn to get along.”

Sutton said [[Idaho Republican Party|Republican]] voters need to be more aware and involved in the election of [[precinct committeemen|precinct committee officers]] throughout the [[State of Idaho|state]] to influence the direction of the [[Idaho Republican Party|state GOP party]].

“If we can get back into a larger majority of those [[precinct committeemen]] throughout the [[State of Idaho|state]] taking a more moderate stance, then we’ll have the ability to stand up against that (current) voting block of the majority (in the [[Idaho Republican Party|Idaho GOP]]),” he said.
