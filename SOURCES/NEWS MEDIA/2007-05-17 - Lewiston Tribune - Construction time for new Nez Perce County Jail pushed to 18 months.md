Estimated construction time on a new [[Nez Perce County Jail]] was extended to about 18 months Wednesday because of concerns not enough time was being allowed.

Sixty days were added to bring the contract time to 540 calendar days because several contractors said work would start during their busy season, [[Lewiston]] architect [[Leonard Ellis]] said after a pre-bid conference at the Brammer Building.

Bids on the 61,674-square-foot [[jails|jail]] will be opened at 2 p.m. May 31, also in the Brammer Building main floor conference room. It will have 156 inmate beds, and the [[County Sheriff|sheriff]]'s office and driver's license office also will be relocated from the center of downtown to the North [[Lewiston]] site.

The 156-bed project is estimated at a total of $19.75 million, but that includes more than construction of the building with its intricate network of electronic security equipment. Included in the lump sum are [[architects]], [[financing]] costs, purchase of 10 acres from the [[Port of Lewiston]], land preparation, an access road, and parking lot.

[[Leonard Ellis]] of the [[Lewiston]] firm of [[Ellis Feeney Architects]] said he has called more contractors in more disciplines than on any other project he's ever been associated with to make sure they know about the project.

The more people who bid, he said, the better deal the [[counties|county]] is likely to get.

Copies of the jail drawings and specifications also have been placed at plan centers across the country because many construction companies have national affiliations, he said. Most plan centers also make them available on the [[Internet]], which gives general and subcontractors easy access.

With two weeks until bid opening, six [[general contractors]] have picked up plans, Ellis said, plus seven have opted for just the mechanical portion, six for the electrical work, three each for security electronics and detention hardware, and various numbers for other aspects of the job.

Four of the general contractors were represented at the Wednesday meeting, which wasn't mandatory in order to submit a bid, Ellis said.

The [[jails|jail]] is being financed by the sale of certificates of participation, which are similar to bonds except they will be repaid through lease payments instead of [[property tax|property taxes]]. The county will lease the [[jails|jail]] from the financial institution until the final payment is made, and then ownership will revert to the [[counties|county]].

[[Nez Perce County|The county]] will make the lease payments using income from a [[local option sales tax]] approved by voters three years ago. The half-cent [[sales tax]] is split evenly between [[jails|jail]] construction and [[property tax relief]].
