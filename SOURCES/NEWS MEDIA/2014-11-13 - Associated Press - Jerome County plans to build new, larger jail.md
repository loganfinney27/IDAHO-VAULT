[[JEROME]] -- Officials in [[Jerome County]] in [[Magic Valley|south-central Idaho]] plan to break ground on a new [[jails|jail]] and [[County Sheriff|sheriff]]'s office in March after voters finally approved an $11.2 million [[bonds|bond]].

The [[Magic Valley Times-News|Times-News]] reports that the new [[jails|jail]] will have 135 beds with room to expand.

The current [[jails|jail]] has 32 beds but up to 80 [[inmates]] at a time. The [[counties|county]] must pay to house the extra [[inmates]] in neighboring [[jails]].

Official say the new [[jails|jail]] will eliminate that problem and save the [[Jerome County|county]] $250,000 a year.

Voters approved the [[bonds|bond]] in May 2013 after rejecting it in 2009, 2010 and 2012.
